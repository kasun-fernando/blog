I am a mathematician, math educator, and a machine learning & data science enthusiast.

Currently, I am a Junior Visitor in Dynamical Systems at the Centro di Ricerca Matematica Ennio De Giorgi, Scuola Normale Superiore di Pisa in Italy.

I completed my PhD in Mathematics at the University of Maryland in the US under the supervision of Dmitry Dolgopyat in August 2018 with the dissertation: Higher Order Asymptotics for the Central Limit Theorem and Large Deviation Principles. From August 2018 to July 2021, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Toronto in Canada where I continued my research work in chaotic dynamical systems & Markov processes, and branched out into inferential statistics & machine learning.

Apart from research work, I am actively involved in the mathematical community as an educator. Due to my commitment to teaching, in 2020, I won the Award for Teaching Excellence by a Junior Member of the Mathematics Department, University of Toronto, Mississauga. Also, since the beginning of my undergraduate studies, I have been contributing to many outreach projects in Canada, Sri Lanka, and the US with the hope of popularizing mathematics among the general public and promoting diversity in STEM.
